Starknet Market Outlook: On-Chain Signals Worth Watching

🗓️ 2026-04-07 🔥 Trending: RedStone, Pudgy Penguins, Bitgert, Celestia, EigenLayer, Monad, institutional DeFi, modular infrastructure, 2026 technical roadmap

Starknet Market Outlook: On-Chain Signals Worth Watching

Starknet’s rollup has experienced a rollercoaster of optimism and skepticism this year. One moment, the network boasts sub-second finality; the next, delayed L2-to-L1 proofs trigger withdrawals, rattling confidence. This tension isn’t just technical—it’s a market narrative investors are closely monitoring.

The current moment is particularly interesting due to the convergence of on-chain signals hinting at deeper structural shifts. From oracle adoption to meme-driven sentiment, these data points are noisy but collectively suggest a possible inflection point for Starknet’s 2026 technical roadmap.

Starknet and RedStone: Oracle Synergy in the 2026 Technical Roadmap

RedStone, a rapidly growing oracle provider with over $10 billion in yield-bearing collateral, is being piloted on Starknet’s emerging lending modules. RedStone offers a variety of in-demand assets, such as LSTs, LRTs, and Bitcoin LSTs, to clients like Spark, Morpho, Compound, and Lido. This move aligns with Starknet’s roadmap emphasis on modular, high-throughput infrastructure.

Pudgy Penguins: Meme Capital as a Sentiment Indicator

PENGU is the official coin of Pudgy Penguins, a popular Ethereum NFT collection with a community token. Pudgy Penguins has become a cultural phenomenon, with one of the most influential communities in the industry. PENGU allows the Pudgy Penguin fanbase and hundreds of millions of people outside of crypto to join The Huddle. PENGU represents community and memes.

Starknet’s community forums have seen a surge in PENGU memes, a subtle but measurable signal that the network’s user base is aligning with broader pop-culture trends. Tracking PENGU’s on-chain metrics could serve as a low-cost proxy for sentiment swings that precede larger capital movements into Starknet-based projects.

Bitgert and the Quest for Low-Cost Gas on Modular Chains

Bitgert (BRISE) positions itself as a low-fee alternative. However, claims about its bridge security model and integration timeline require source support.

Celestia’s Data Availability Layer and Starknet’s Rollup Evolution

Celestia’s modular data availability service can be evaluated by Starknet’s engineering team as a potential layer-2 scaling enhancer. By offloading calldata to Celestia, Starknet could reduce on-chain data costs and improve throughput, aligning with the network’s 2026 roadmap focus on modularity.

EigenLayer’s Restaking Model: Modular Infrastructure

EigenLayer’s restaking paradigm allows validators to lock up existing staking assets to secure additional services. Early data from EigenLayer shows a modest uptick in restaked ETH. The alignment between EigenLayer’s modular security and Starknet’s ambition for a composable, multi-chain ecosystem is an area of interest.

Monad, a parallelized EVM Layer 1, is another project worth watching. Its mainnet launch is reportedly on track, and its parallel execution mechanism could enhance Starknet’s performance.

Ethereum restaking primitive EigenLayer and Starknet share a common goal of creating a more modular and secure ecosystem. As institutional DeFi continues to grow, the need for robust infrastructure will only increase.

The convergence of these on-chain signals and technical developments suggests that Starknet may be poised for significant growth in 2026. With its focus on modularity and high-throughput infrastructure, Starknet is well-positioned to capitalize on the emerging trends in the crypto space.

As the market continues to evolve, it will be essential to monitor these on-chain signals and technical developments to gain a deeper understanding of Starknet’s potential. By doing so, investors and users can make more informed decisions about their involvement with the network.
