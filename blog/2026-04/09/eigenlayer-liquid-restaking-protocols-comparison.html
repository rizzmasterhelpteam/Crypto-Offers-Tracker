Restaking Mechanics Explained

Traditional staking locks capital. Liquid restaking tokens (LRTs) solve this by issuing a tradable, yield-bearing asset that can be used in DeFi while the underlying ETH secures additional services via EigenLayer.

Top EigenLayer LRT Protocols in 2026

The leading liquid restaking protocols include:

  • Ether.fi (eETH / weETH) — Largest LRT by TVL, strong DeFi integrations, often used as collateral.
  • Renzo (ezETH) — Aggressive AVS exposure, multi-chain elements, 10% fee on restaking rewards.
  • Kelp DAO (rsETH) — Supports multiple LSTs, good flexibility across EVM networks.

These protocols handle operator curation and AVS selection, making restaking accessible without manual delegation.

Risks of Liquid Restaking in 2026

  • Slashing Risk: Operators can be penalized for AVS misbehavior. EigenLayer has introduced better isolation via Operator Sets and Unique Stake Allocation to reduce systemic impact.
  • Smart Contract Risk: Multiple layers of contracts increase attack surface.
  • Depeg & Liquidity Risk: LRTs like ezETH have experienced temporary depegs in the past.
  • Opportunity Cost & Volatility: High advertised yields often come with higher risk.

Pro Tip: Stick to top TVL LRTs, diversify operators, and start with small positions.

FAQs – EigenLayer Liquid Restaking 2026

Is liquid restaking safe?

It adds risks (slashing, smart contracts) compared to native staking. Use reputable LRTs and never invest more than you can afford to lose.

How do LRT yields compare to regular staking?

LRTs add variable AVS rewards on top of ~3-4% ETH staking, but actual net APY is often 3.5–7%+ depending on market conditions.

Should I choose EigenLayer or JitoSOL?

EigenLayer for Ethereum composability and diversified security. JitoSOL for faster transactions and MEV in the Solana ecosystem.

Bottom Line: EigenLayer liquid restaking continues to offer innovative ways to enhance ETH yields in 2026, but success depends on understanding variable rewards and managing added risks. Start small, monitor closely, and treat it as part of a diversified DeFi strategy.
