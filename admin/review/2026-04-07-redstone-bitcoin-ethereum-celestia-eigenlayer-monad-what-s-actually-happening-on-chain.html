RedStone, Bitcoin, Ethereum, Celestia, EigenLayer, Monad: What's Actually Happening On-Chain

🗓️ 2026-04-07 🔥 Trending: RedStone, Bitcoin, Ethereum, Celestia, EigenLayer, Monad, institutional DeFi, modular infrastructure, 2026 technical roadmap

Blockchain activity is increasingly driven by hard data, with notable developments including a $10 bn oracle pipeline, a 21‑million‑coin settlement layer that still processes transactions daily, a programmable base layer, and a suite of modular services.

RedStone and Bitcoin: Data Feeds

RedStone operates as a pull-based oracle model [per CoinGecko]. Bitcoin continues to enforce its 21 million‑coin cap and quarterly halvings, maintaining a deflationary issuance schedule [per CoinGecko].

Ethereum, Celestia, and Modular Infrastructure

Ethereum's ecosystem is evolving with the integration of modular data availability layers. Celestia, a modular DA layer, utilizes Data Availability Sampling (DAS) and Namespace Merkle Trees (NMTs) [per Celestia docs]. This quarter, Celestia is focused on expansion beyond Ethereum L3s toward native modular alignment with Avail and internal throughput optimizations [per TIA roadmap].

EigenLayer and Verifiable Cloud

EigenLayer has emerged as a restaked security marketplace, with over $18B+ TVL as of February 2026 [per EigenLayer metrics]. The protocol is focused on Vertical AVS (EigenAI, EigenCompute) and Multichain Verification across L2s [per EIGEN roadmap].

Monad and Parallel Execution

Monad, a parallel EVM L1, launched its mainnet in November 2025, achieving 10k TPS with 0.4s block times [per MONAD docs]. The protocol's 2026 focus includes the MONAD_NINE upgrade, optimized for memory costs and EIP-7823 alignment [per Monad AI Blueprint].
